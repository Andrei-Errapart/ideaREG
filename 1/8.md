## Idea

### Title

**USB-to-Anything Bridge using TE0790 Hardware** — repurposing existing commercial JTAG adapter hardware as a general multi-protocol bridge through alternative FPGA firmware.

### Description

The Trenz Electronic [TE0790](https://www.trenz-electronic.de/en/XMOD-FTDI-JTAG-Adapter-AMD-compatible/TE0790-03) is a commercially available USB JTAG adapter compatible with AMD (Xilinx) FPGAs. The board contains an FTDI FT2232H dual-channel high-speed USB controller and a Lattice FPGA. In its standard commercial firmware, it provides JTAG programming and UART pass-through capabilities for FPGA development workflows.

The hardware on this board is more capable than its standard firmware exposes. The FT2232H supports a wide range of protocols through its MPSSE (Multi-Protocol Synchronous Serial Engine), and the Lattice FPGA is a general-purpose programmable logic device that can be configured to implement many different protocol bridges. The standard JTAG firmware uses only a small fraction of what the hardware could do.

This idea is to develop alternative firmware for the TE0790's Lattice FPGA, turning the commercially available board into a **general USB-to-anything bridge**. The same hardware, with different firmware, would support:

- **I2C** — master-mode bridge for I2C peripheral access from the host PC
- **SPI** — master-mode bridge for SPI peripheral access, including SPI flash programming and general device interfacing
- **1-Wire** — master for Dallas/Maxim 1-Wire device access
- **Generic synchronous serial** — bit-level timing control for protocols not directly covered by FTDI's MPSSE
- **Custom logic-analyser front-end** — capturing signals at moderate speeds for protocol debugging
- **GPIO with timing control** — controlled signalling on individual pins for bit-bang protocols

The host-side software would expose these capabilities through standard interfaces — libftdi for direct MPSSE access, Linux kernel modules for protocol-specific access (i2c-dev, spidev, w1), or a unified library that handles protocol switching automatically.

The benefit to engineers is real. A single device that handles JTAG, UART, I2C, SPI, 1-Wire, and other protocols, at the price point of the existing TE0790, would replace the mix of separate tools that engineers currently use for these tasks (Bus Pirate, FT232H breakout boards, dedicated protocol dongles, FTDI cables, and others).

### Why this idea matters

**The hardware is already commercially available.** The TE0790 is in production at Trenz Electronic and can be purchased today. No new hardware development is required. The work is firmware and software development, which is substantially less expensive and time-consuming than producing new hardware.

**The expanded functionality benefits all parties.** Trenz Electronic benefits because more capable firmware would increase the appeal of their existing hardware. Engineers benefit because a single device replaces several. The broader FPGA development community benefits from having a well-supported general-purpose USB-to-anything bridge available.

**The pattern is generalisable.** Other commercial JTAG adapters and FTDI-based development tools have similar architectural flexibility — FT2232H plus user-programmable logic. The same firmware development approach could be applied to similar boards from other vendors. The TE0790 is one specific example; the broader pattern of *use existing JTAG-adapter hardware as a multi-protocol bridge* applies more generally.

### Domain or field

USB device development, FTDI MPSSE programming, FPGA firmware development, protocol bridges, embedded development tooling.

### References to related ideas

- **Bus Pirate** (Dangerous Prototypes) — multi-protocol USB bridge with similar functionality, but limited by its fixed hardware design.
- **FT232H breakout boards** (Adafruit, SparkFun, others) — FTDI-based protocol bridges with user firmware control, but without the FPGA layer that TE0790 provides.
- **Dedicated protocol-specific dongles** (Total Phase Aardvark for I2C/SPI, various 1-Wire dongles) — single-protocol commercial products with higher cost than a general-purpose multi-protocol bridge would have.
- **FTDI's own MPSSE example projects** — demonstrate what FT2232H can do without external logic, but do not address use cases requiring user-programmable logic alongside the FTDI chip.

The specific contribution of this registration is the application of the FT2232H + FPGA architecture pattern to existing commercial JTAG-adapter hardware (TE0790), through firmware development that unlocks capabilities the hardware can support but the standard firmware does not expose.

## Remarks

This idea differs from typical ideaREG registrations in that the underlying hardware already exists as a commercial product. The work proposed here is firmware and software development against existing hardware, not new hardware development. This makes the idea unusually feasible — anyone with TE0790 hardware and Lattice FPGA development experience could begin work on the firmware immediately.

**Trenz Electronic's interests are relevant.** Trenz makes and sells the TE0790. They may welcome third-party firmware development that increases the appeal of their hardware, or they may prefer to be the primary firmware provider for their product. Anyone pursuing this idea seriously should consult with Trenz Electronic about their preferences and the licensing terms under which TE0790 firmware development is permitted.

The author of this registration has no formal relationship with Trenz Electronic relevant to this idea, beyond knowing of the TE0790 product and recognising its hardware capability.

The MIT license under which this registration is made allows free use, modification, and commercial development by any party. However, the firmware development against the TE0790 itself would be subject to whatever terms Trenz Electronic establishes for development against their hardware (which may include their own IP in the Lattice FPGA configuration, schematic restrictions, or other relevant considerations).

The pattern described here applies more broadly than the TE0790 specifically. Other FTDI-based JTAG adapters with user-programmable logic could be approached the same way. Any engineer pursuing this direction could choose the specific hardware platform that best fits their needs, with the TE0790 being one option among several.
